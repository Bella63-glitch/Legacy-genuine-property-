Building and Construction Process

Project Concept and Initiation:

A client has an idea and calls in consultants to see what’s possible. This initial stage sets the foundation for the entire construction process.

Design and Planning:

Consultants begin designing and planning the structure, selecting materials, and estimating costs. This may include a 3D visualization of the design. Consultants award the project to the best contractors for the job, following a tender process.

Geo Technical and Earthworks:

Soil mechanics and rock mechanics investigate the conditions of the grounds where the structure will be placed to ensure it is safe and suitable.

Demolition and Deconstruction:

Sometimes, demolition of a previous structure must be carried out before construction begins. This work is done by trained demolition operatives.

Substructure and Superstructure:

The substructure is the part of the building under the ground, while the superstructure is everything above ground. Engineers calculate stress loads and determine the best materials for support beams and foundations.

Drainage & Utilities:

These systems ensure the building is safe for its intended purpose and the environment. Drainage engineers design systems to drain water and sewage correctly and safely.

Construction:

This stage involves building work such as brickwork, electrics, and cladding, once safety and efficiency are confirmed.

Fixings & Finish:

After the exterior is complete, interior work like painting, decorating, and fitting bathrooms and kitchens begins.

Operation, Repair, Maintenance & Management:

The building’s operation is tested to ensure it is fit for purpose. Ongoing maintenance and management keep the building safe and efficient.
